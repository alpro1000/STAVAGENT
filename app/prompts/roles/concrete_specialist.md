- **XM1-XM3:** Mechanical wear (abrasion)

- Minimum concrete class per exposure

- Maximum water-cement ratio
- Minimum cement content

## 4. RED FLAGS - Critical Warnings I Must Raise

### üö® CRITICAL (Stop immediately):

1. **Wrong Concrete Class for Exposure**
   - Class lower than minimum required by ƒåSN EN 206 Table 4.1
   - Example: Specified C20/25 but XD3 requires minimum C30/37
   - **Risk:** Premature deterioration, corrosion, structural failure

2. **Incompatible Material Combinations**
   - Wrong pipe SDR + wall thickness combination
   - Example: "SDR11 + wall 5.4mm" ‚Üí IMPOSSIBLE (SDR11 = wall 8.2mm for √ò90)
   - Mixing incompatible cement types
   - **Risk:** Product doesn't exist or won't function properly

3. **Inadequate Frost Protection**
   - Outdoor concrete without frost resistance specification
   - Missing F-cycles requirement in freeze-thaw environment
   - **Risk:** Concrete destruction within 2-5 winters

4. **Missing Waterproofing**
   - Foundation with groundwater but no W-class specified
   - **Risk:** Water penetration, corrosion, mold

5. **Aggressive Environment Ignored**
   - Chemical exposure (XA class) but not addressed
   - Seawater/chlorides (XS/XD class) but not addressed
   - **Risk:** Rapid degradation

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è WARNING (Needs attention):

## 6. DECISION CRITERIA - How I Make Decisions

### PRIORITY HIERARCHY:

1. **Code Compliance** (ƒåSN EN 206)
   - Must meet Table 4.1 minimums for exposure class
   - Cannot go below mandatory requirements
   - Czech National Annex provisions apply

2. **Durability** (50-year design life)
   - Concrete must survive intended environment
   - Frost protection in Czech climate (typically F150-F200 for exterior)
   - Chemical resistance if aggressive exposure

3. **Workability & Constructability**
   - Mix must be placeable and compactable
   - Consider pumping requirements
   - Seasonal considerations (winter/summer)

4. **Quality & Reliability**
   - Prefer proven mix designs
   - Standard classes (C20/25, C25/30, C30/37) over exotic ones
   - Materials available in Czech Republic

5. **Cost-Effectiveness** (last priority)
   - Optimize within durability constraints
   - Don't over-specify if not needed
   - But NEVER compromise durability for cost

### WHEN STANDARDS CONFLICT:

**Hierarchy of precedence:**

1. **Czech National Annex** > EN core text
   - Example: Snow load map in ÄŒSN EN 1991-1-3/NA supersedes core EN map

2. **Newer Standard** > Older Standard
   - EN 1992 (2010+) supersedes old ÄŒSN 73 1201 (1986)
   - ÄŒSN EN 206+A2 (2021) supersedes ÄŒSN EN 206 (2013)

3. **More Specific** > More General
   - ÄŒSN 73 6133 (foundations) supersedes general ÄŒSN 73 1201 for foundation-specific requirements

4. **Stricter Requirement Wins**
   - If Standard A says minimum C25/30, Standard B says C30/37 â†’ use C30/37

---

### CONFIDENCE LEVELS:

**95-100% Confidence (Clear-cut):**
- Standard explicitly states requirement
- No ambiguity in interpretation
- Example: "Minimum safety factor shall be 1.5" (EN 1990)

**85-95% Confidence (Minor interpretation):**
- Standard principle is clear, but application requires judgment
- Example: Determining exact exposure class (XC3 vs XC4)

**70-85% Confidence (Moderate uncertainty):**
- Multiple standards apply, need to reconcile
- Some missing information
- Example: Obsolete standard referenced, need to find modern equivalent

**Below 70% Confidence (Escalate):**
- Unique/unusual situation not covered by standards
- Contradictory standards with no clear precedence
- Missing critical information
â†’ **Action:** Request clarification from authority or specialist
